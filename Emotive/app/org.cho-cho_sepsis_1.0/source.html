Pediatric Severe Sepsis/Septic Shock Assessment

  • Rapid Sepsis Assessment Tool
  • Initially, pediatric patients present with:
    • Low or high temperature
    • Tachycardia
    • Altered mental status
    • Poor perfusion or FLASH capillary refill
  • Hypotension is LATE FINDING!

Lab testing

  • POC tests-blood gas, glucose, lytes, lactate*
  • CBC, chem 8
  • Cultures: Blood, ETT if intubated (Consider Cath Urine and CSF if hemodynamics OK)
  • (NB. Do not delay transport for LP)
  • *Capillary, venous, arterial OK, preferably not IO

0-15 Minutes Overview

If no IV access by 5 minutes, place IO!

PUSH IV fluids

Assess point of care results

  • Treat hypoglycemia and hypocalcemia

Order antibiotics and give ASAP

  • Goal: First doses IN by 30 minutes!

Order Inotropes/Vasopressors to bedside

Fluids

  • NS, Plasmalyte, LR or 5% albumin by hand over 5 minutes, not on a pump
  • Start with 20 ml/kg IV
  • Repeat until perfusion improves unless rales or hepatomegaly develop
  • Assess liver margin before and after each NS bolus
  • Likely will need at least 60 mL/kg and may need up to 100 mL/kg!

Assessment of Labs

  • If ionized Ca < 1
    • Calcium Chloride 10 mg/kg IV over 5-15 min. May repeat x 1
    • If no CaCl, use Calcium Gluconate 50 mg/kg IV over 5-15 min. May repeat x 1
    • Watch CLOSELY for extravasation!!
  • If pH < 7.0, consider NaHCO3 1 mEq/kg. May repeat x 1
  • If glucose < 40 or seizing from hypoglycemia
    • 4 mL/kg D10W IVP OR
    • 1 mL/kg D50W IVP (watch IV site CLOSELY)
    • Repeat POC glucose in 15 min and again in 60 min!

Antibiotics for Severe Sepsis/Septic Shock

  • Age 0-28 days: Ampicillin 50 mg/kg IVP and Cefotaxime 50 mg/kg IVP
  • > 28 days: Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IVP or IM
  • (Consider Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV over 30 minutes for presumed Staph aureus. Discuss with PICU physician if able.)

Inotropes/Vasopressors

  • Use if BP low despite fluid resuscitation (at least 60 mL/kg) or evidence of hepatomegaly or resp distress
  • Place second IV if able
  • MAY give inotropes/vasopressors through PIV or IO
  • Choices:
    • 1. Dopamine 5-20 mcg/kg/min, esp for use in PIV
    • 2. Epinephrine 0.1-0.3 mcg/kg/min (if cold extremities) OR
    • Norepinephrine 0.1-0.3 mcg/kg/min (if warm extremities)

15-60 Minutes Overview

REASSESS!

Consider hydrocortisone for potential adrenal insufficiency!

Reconsider need for inotropes/vasopressors if not already being given.

Items to Reassess

  • Have appropriate cultures have been drawn? (min. 1 mL for blood cultures)
  • Have antibiotics been given?
  • Sufficient fluid resuscitation given?
    • Children often need 80 mL/kg - 100 mL/kg for sepsis
  • Reassess CNS and respiratory status!

Antibiotics for Severe Sepsis/Septic Shock

  • Age 0-28 days: Ampicillin 50 mg/kg IVP and Cefotaxime 50 mg/kg IVP
  • > 28 days: Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IVP or IM
  • (Consider Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV over 30 minutes for presumed Staph aureus. Discuss with PICU physician if able.)

Hydrocortisone for adrenal insufficiency*

  • Slow IV push
    • 25 mg IV under 6 months
    • 50 mg IV up to 9 years
    • 100 mg IV if 10 years or older
  • *Indicated if patient has already received MULTIPLE fluid boluses AND inotropes/vasopressors AND is still hypotensive. OR if purpura fulminans

1-4 Hours

  • If BP still low, HR still high or still on inotropes, call PICU MD and consider:
    • Repeat fluid bolus (up to 200 mL/kg)
    • Increase dopamine/epi
    • Add norepinephrine or vasopressin
    • Blood transfusion (10-20 mL/kg)
      • 1 unit = 250-300 mL
      • O neg if emergency
    • Other important things!

Other important things!

  • Resp status
    • ? Need CXR
    • Look for pulm edema/resp distress
    • May need intubation - call PICU MD
  • Pericardial effusion, pneumothorax, increased abd pressure - If so, call PICU MD
  • Need for central access
  • Repeat labs (POC blood gas with lytes, Glu, and Hgb)
