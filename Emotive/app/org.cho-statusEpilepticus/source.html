Do Not Miss!

Resume Protocol
  • Paralytic agents mask ongoing seizure activity
  • Prolonged seizures (> 30 mins) may result in hypoglycemia, acidosis and hyperpyrexia
  • Consider Head CT if:
    • History of or external evidence of trauma and/or
    • Infant without external evidence of trauma (R/O abuse)
  • OK

Time Zero

  • Assess, treat, stabilize per protocol
  • Perform and record neurological exams at least every 5 minutes
  • Indications for Intubation
  • Consider head CT if:
    • Evidence of trauma
    • Infant (<1 year of age)
  • Neurosurgical intervention, if available, may be required prior to transport

Neurological Exams

  • Level of consciousness
  • Glasgow Coma Scale
  • Pupil equality, size and reactivity
  • Gaze conjugate or disconjugate
  • BP

Indications for Intubation

  • SpO2 < 94% despite optimal non- invasive support
  • Loss of gag reflex
  • Clinical signs of herniation: Cushing's= ↑BP + ↓HR + irreg. resp.
  • Avoid paralysis during intubation if possible to avoid masking seizures
    • If paralysis necessary for pt safety, monitor for: ↑BP, ↓SpO2, pupil dilation (signs of seizure)

Fluids and Electrolytes

Check blood glucose q 30 min until seizure stopped. Continue checking hourly x 4 hours once stable

Check Na

Check iCa (ionized calcium)

Use EPOC for lytes. Expect acidosis. Consider fluid bolus.

Glucose

  • Check BS (blood glucose) q 30 min until seizure stopped, then q 1 hr
  • If BS > 150
    • Use NS as IVF
  • If BS < 150 and > 80
    • Use D5 NS as IVF
  • If BS < 80
    • Give D25 2 mL/kg slow IVP, then D5 NS as IVF

    Check Na

    • Na < 120
      • Give 6 mL/kg 3% NaCl over 10 min.
      • If 3% NaCl unavailable, give NS 20 mL/kg
    • Na < 130 and ongoing seizure after phenytoin
      • Give 6 mL/kg 3% NaCl over 10 min.
      • If 3% NaCl unavailable, give NS 20 mL/kg
    • Check Na after bolus and repeat 3% NaCl as above prn ↓Na

    Check iCa++ (ionized Calcium)

    • If iCa++ < 0.90
      • Ca gluconate 50 mg/kg IV over 2 min.

    Neurologic

    If seizing,

    • Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg slow IV push (max 2 mg)
      • May repeat in 5 min prn seizure activity x 3
    • Monitor respiratory status and BP. Consult MD if BP > 150

    If still seizing

    Ongoing seizure

    • Fosphenytoin (or phenytoin*) 20 mg/kg IV over 20 minutes
    • Wait 5 minutes. Ensure glucose, Na, and iCa are corrected. If still seizing, repeat.
    • Fosphenytoin or phenytoin 10 mg/kg
    • *Phenytoin may result in arrhythmia or precipitate PIV. Use with caution.
    • If patient currently taking phenobarbital, phenytoin and/or valproate: check level STAT

    If still seizing

    Indications for Intubation

    • SpO2 < 94% despite optimal non- invasive support
    • Loss of gag reflex
    • Clinical signs of herniation: Cushing's= ↑BP + ↓HR + irreg. resp.
    • Avoid paralysis during intubation if possible to avoid masking seizures
      • If paralysis necessary for pt safety, monitor for: ↑BP, ↓SpO2, pupil dilation (signs of seizure)
    • Ensure adequate sedation and analgesia

    Sedation and analgesia

    • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg or morphine 0.1 mg/kg
    • Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg (max 5 mg) or lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg (max 2 mg)
    • Avoid propofol due to risk of hypotension
    • Avoid paralytics if clinically possible
    • If patient currently taking phenobarbital, phenytoin and/or valproate: check level STAT

    If still seizing,

    • Contact PICU. Fourth line treatments include:
      • Levetiracetam (Keppra)
      • Valproic Acid (Depakon)
      • Midazolam infusion
    • Ensure adequate BP and respiratory status

    Levetiracetam (Keppra)

    • 20 mg/kg load IV (if patient on this drug already, consider 5mg/kg/load)
    • Dilute dose in 100 mL NS, LR, or D5W and infuse over 15 minutes

    Valproic Acid (Depakon)

    • 20-40 mg/kg load IV (if patient on this drug already, send stat level and consider 5mg/kg load)
    • Dilute dose in 50 mL of NS, LR, or D5W and infuse over 60 minutes

    Midazolam infusion

    • Midazolam infusion at 0.1 mg/kg/hr IV
    • Dilute 5 mg/mL formulation to concentration of 0.5 mg/mL with NS or D5W

    Respiratory

    • Goal SpO2 > 94%
      • One instance of hypoxia has significant negative impact on outcome. Continuously ensure adequate oxygenation
    • If intubated
      • Goal EtCO2 35-45mm Hg
      • Minimize mean airway pressure. Maximize oxygenation.
        • Note: physiologic PEEP (3-5) is preferred.

    Indications for Intubation

    • SpO2 < 94% despite optimal non- invasive support
    • Loss of gag reflex
    • Clinical signs of herniation: Cushing's= ↑BP + ↓HR + irreg. resp.
    • Avoid paralysis during intubation if possible to avoid masking seizures
      • If paralysis necessary for pt safety, monitor for: ↑BP, ↓SpO2, pupil dilation (signs of seizure)

    Cardiovascular

    Maintain BP

    • Age < 1, MAP > 50
    • Age 1-8 yrs, MAP > 70
    • Age ≥ 8, MAP > 80
    • If MAP < goal, push NS 20 mL/kg. Repeat X 1 as needed for MAP < goal
    • Consider dopamine (5-10 mcg/kg/min)

    Fever

    • Treat hyperthermia - Fever decreases seizure threshold and makes status epilepticus harder to stop.
    • Acetaminophen 15 mg/kg NG or PR or IV
    • Is infection suspected?

    Infection

    • If infection suspected, verify blood cultures have been sent and LP done.
    • If LP done, request 1 CSF tube to bring on transport.
    • Do not do LP in unstable patient!
    • Verify antibiotics have been given. If not, consider Antibiotics Call MD for consult.
    • Transport personnel should assume contact and droplet precautions.

    Antibiotics

    • Ceftriaxone recommended dose of 100 mg/kg IVP
    • If patient has VP shunt, consider Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV over 60 min
    • If infant with bloody CSF, consider Acyclovir 20 mg/kg IV over 60 min

    Positioning

    • Elevate HOB 30-45 degrees
    • Keep head midline to promote venous drainage
