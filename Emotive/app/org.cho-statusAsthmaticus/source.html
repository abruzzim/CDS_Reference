Do Not Miss!

Start New Protocol
Resume Protocol
  • ↓ or no BS, ↓RR and/or ↓HR seen with impending respiratory failure
  • Extreme agitation OR obtundation seen with severe hypoxemia
  • ↑↑HR IS expected. Minimal risk of MI with β agonist use compared to adults
  • Allow patient to assume position of comfort (usually tripod NOT supine) to allow for accessory muscle usage
  • OK

Initial Assessment

Degree of Distress:

    Severe

    Moderate

    Mild

Severe Distress

  • Restlessness/agitation, or lethargy
  • Severe retractions
  • Head bobbing
  • Absent or minimal wheezes
  • Abnormally low RR
  • Unable to speak
  • Next

Severe Distress

  • Support Airway
    • 100% Oxygen
    • If CPAP/Bi-PAP available
      • PEEP 7-10 cm H2O
      • PIP: 12-18 cm H2O
  • Next

Severe Distress

  • Patient will be very anxious.
  • Sit on bed with patient. Look at patient directly and tell them they are breathing and you will keep them safe.
  • Start by placing Ambu Bag CPAP on face. Once tolerated, tell patient you are quickly transitioning to BiPap/CPAP mask.
  • Next

Severe Distress

  • If obtunded, severely hypoxic or ↓HR:
    • Intubate
    • CBG or ABG via EPOC
    • Use RR 5-15 (need long E time)
    • Liberal sedation and paralysis
      • Do not allow spontaneous respiration (high risk of barotrauma)
    • Continue albuterol/steroids

Severe Distress

  • Continuous albuterol
    • < 10 kg: 10 mg/hr
    • 10-20 kg: 15 mg/hr
    • > 20 kg: 20 mg/hr
  • Next

Severe Distress

  • IV corticosteroids
    • Methylprednisolone 2 mg/kg (max 125 mg)
    • OR
    • Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg IV (max 10 mg)
    • NOTE: If steroids have already been given orally, consider re-dosing IV as above
  • Next

Severe Distress

  • Magnesium Sulfate IV x 1
    • 50 mg/kg (25-100 mg/kg) IV over 20 min (max 2 gm)
  • Terbutaline infusion at 1mcg/kg/min
    • May ↑ by 0.5 mcg/kg/min q 30 min (max 4 mcg/kg/min)
    • NOTE: Pediatric doses >> adult doses
  • Next

Severe Distress

  • If inadequate chest movement and no improvement in ETCO2, consider:
    • Suction ETT
    • Asthma patients can have severe mucus casting
    • Primary objective is adequate oxygenation
    • Ventilation may remain poor after intubation

Moderate Distress

  • Infants
    • Softer, shorter cry
    • Difficulty feeding
    • ↑RR
    • Accessory muscle use
    • Inspiratory/expiratory wheezes
  • Children/Adolescents
    • Prefer sitting upright
    • Short phrases, one word answers
    • Inspiratory/expiratory wheezes
  • Next

Moderate Distress

  • Place PIV
    • May attempt x 3
    • Use IM or SQ meds if IV difficult
      • Prolonged IV attempts worsen resp. status
    • Have IO available
  • O2 to keep sats ≥ 92%
  • Next

Moderate Distress

  • High dose inhaled albuterol:
    • START WITH:
      • If < 30 kg, give 2.5 mg aerosolized albuterol q 10 min x 3 (Combineb ok)
      • If ≥ 30 kg, give 5 mg aerosolized albuterol q 10 min x 3 (Combineb ok)
    • THEN consider: Continuous albuterol
      • If < 10 kg, then 10 mg/hr
      • If 10-20 kg, then 15 mg/hr
      • If > 20 kg, then 20 mg/hr
  • Next

Moderate Distress

  • Prefer IV corticosteroids:
    • Methylprednisolone 1 mg/kg IV (max 125 mg) OR
    • Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg IV (max 10 mg)
    • If no IV access, may use Prednisone 2 mg/kg po (max 60 mg) OR Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg IM (max 10 mg)
  • Ipratropium Bromide (1.5 ml) aerosolized with albuterol
  • NS bolus 20 mL/kg IV
    • May repeat x 1 if perfusion poor
  • Next

Moderate Distress

  • If no improvement in WOB or distress:
    • Magnesium Sulfate x 1
      • 50 mg/kg (25-100 mg/kg) IV over 20 min (max 2 gm)
    • Terbutaline - 0.1 mg/kg sq (max 0.25 mg) q 5' x 3 prn distress
    • LEAST PREFERRED: SQ Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.5 mg)
      • May repeat q 20' x 3 prn distress
      • NOTE: thigh muscles preferred over arm
  • If still no improvement in WOB or distress

Moderate Distress

  • If still no improvement in WOB or distress:
    • Terbutaline infusion at 1 mcg/kg/min
      • NOTE: Pediatric doses >> recommended adult doses
    • Assess for IMPENDING RESPIRATORY FAILURE

Mild Distress

  • Shortness of breath with activity
  • Able to lie flat
  • Speaks in complete sentences
  • Normal mental status
  • Stranger anxiety
  • Moderate wheezing, mostly expiratory
  • HR usually WNL for age

Mild Distress

  • Give oxygen to achieve SpO2 ≥ 92%
    • Nasal cannula O2 @ 1-3 LPM
    • Simple mask @ 6 LPM minimum
  • Inhaled short acting β-agonist
    • < 30 kg: 2.5 mg aerosolized albuterol
    • > 30 kg: 5 mg aerosolized albuterol
    • May repeat up to 3 times in 1 hour
    • CONSIDER levalbuterol (Xopenex) 0.15 mg/kg (max dose 5 mg) nebulized if strong parental preference
  • Next

Mild Distress

  • Oral systemic steroids
    • 1 mg/kg Prednisone po (max 60 mg)
    • OR
    • Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg po (max 10 mg)
  • If no improvement in WOB or distress go to Moderate

Focused History

  • Duration of symptoms
  • Preceding illness
  • Trigger
  • Current home treatment/ home meds and effectiveness

Past History

  • Known asthma
  • Medication/ compliance
  • Number of prior admissions
  • Prior intubation or ICU admission
  • Note: History of intubation or ICU admission is indicator of asthma severity and increased risk of mortality

Focused Physical Exam

  • Vital signs
  • Note: Slow RR can be ominous sign
  • Work of breathing, aeration, wheezing (Inspiratory and/ or Expiratory)
  • Speech (Full sentences, short phrases, single words)
  • Mental status

Labs/Xrays

  • Clinical exam overrides labs
  • Blood gas is NOT required for assessment
  • Note: Attempts at obtaining blood gas can exacerbate distress without improving assessment
  • Note: bNormalb pCO2 with tachypnea is concerning
  • Antibiotics

Antibiotics

  • If infiltrate on CXR or evidence of sepsis, consider:
    • Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IVP AND
    • Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV over 60 min.
  • Now continue to: Moderate or Mild
