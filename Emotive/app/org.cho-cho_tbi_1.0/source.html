    Sedation and analgesia

      • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg or morphine 0.1 mg/kg IV
      • Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg/dose IV
      • Vecuronium 0.1 mg/kg or rocuronium 1 mcg/kg
      • Ensure adequate sedation before giving paralytic!
      • Avoid propofol due to risk of hypotension

    Cardiovascular

    Treat hypotension aggressively

    Assume ICP = 20

    BP/CPP Goals

    If MAP/CPP < goal, push NS 20 mL/kg. Repeat X 1 as needed for MAP < goal

    Consider dopamine or norepinephrine

    Consider PRBC administration if Hgb < 8

    ONE episode of hypotension has negative impact on outcome

    Neurologic Exam

    • Level of consciousness
    • Glasgow Coma Scale
    • Pupil equality, size and reactivity

    Indications for Intubation

    • Persistent SpO2 < 94% despite optimal non-invasive oxygen supplementation
    • ETCO2 > 45 mmHg
    • GCS < 8, motor GCS < 2 or rapidly decreasing GCS
    • Unequal or fixed pupils or disconjugate gaze altered from baseline with altered mental status
    • Absent gag reflex
    • Any clinical signs of herniation: Cushings (High BP, low HR, low RR)
    • Decorticate or decerebrate posturing

    Pressors

    • Start dopamine if MAP less than goal and not responsive to fluid bolus x 2
      • Dose: 5-10 mcg/kg/min
    • Start norepinephrine if concern for spinal shock or if unresponsive to dopamine
      • Dose: 0.05-0.2 mcg/kg/min
    • ONE episode of hypotension has negative impact on outcome
    • DO NOT treat hypertension without consulting receiving MD

    Neurologic

    If signs of herniation

    • Briefly hyperventilate. Consider 3% NaCl or mannitol

    If seizure activity present, treat aggressively

    If intubated, maintain sedation and analgesia and consider paralytic

    Treatment of impending herniation

    • If LOC down, pupillary changes, HR down, BP up
      • Increase RR with goal ETCO2 25-30 mmHg for NO MORE THAN 5 min.
        • Prolonged hyperventilation can cause brain ischemia!
      • 3% NaCl (6mL/kg) or Mannitol (0.5 gm/kg) IV

    Treatment of seizures

    • If seizure activity present
      • Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg or lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV
      • Fosphenytoin or phenytoin 20 mg/kg IV
      • Ensure that IV is patent prior to adminstering phenytoin. Severe burn can result from IV infiltration

    Sedation and analgesia

    • If intubated, maintain sedation and analgesia and consider paralytic
      • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg or morphine 0.1 mg/kg IV
      • Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg/dose IV
      • Vecuronium 0.1 mg/kg or rocuronium 1 mcg/kg
      • Ensure adequate sedation before giving paralytic!
      • Avoid propofol due to risk of hypotension

    Fluids and electrolytes

      Start IV fluids at maintenance

      • NS if glucose > 150 mg/dL
      • D5 NS if glucose between 80 and 150 mg/dL
      • Avoid hypotonic fluids!

      Check blood glucose

      Check Na

    Glucose (mg/dL)

    • Glucose between 80 and 150 mg/dL
      • D5 NS at maintenance
    • Glucose > 150 mg/dL
      • NS at maintenance
    • Glucose < 80 mg/dL
      • D10 5 mL/kg slow push, then D5 NS at maintenance
      • Repeat until Glucose > 80 mg/dL
      • Recheck Glu q 15' during transport

    Check Na

    • If Na < 135 AND 3% NaCl available, give 3% NaCl 6 mL/kg over 60 min.

    Maintenance

    Position patient

    • Elevate head 30-45o
    • Head midline
    • Cervical collar appropriately positioned and not too tight

    Maintain temperature 35-37o C

    Do NOT warm aggressively

    Protect affected body parts from injury

    Temperature

    • Maintain temperature 35-37o C
    • Do NOT warm aggressively
    • Acetaminophen 15 mg/kg ng or pr
    • Continuous temperature monitoring is indicated
    • Avoid hyperthermia
